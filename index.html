BeachCulture

San Lorenzo beach

Turquoise water and fine sand through the hot hours. It's Saturday — book lido sunbeds ahead, free-beach stretches vanish fast.

17:30

Noto baroque walk

Cathedral (open to 20:00), Via Nicolaci, Palazzo Nicolaci — the limestone glows at this hour. Aperitivo and dinner on the corso.

☀

Why this order: Noto at July noon is a white furnace; at golden hour it's the best light of the day, minus the heat and the tour groups.

Sun 26Syracuse, Ortigia and Plemmirio

CultureBeach

9:00

Neapolis archaeological park

Greek theatre and the Ear of Dionysius at opening. In July the cavea is partly rigged for the play season — the evening shows are worth tickets if you can grab them.

11:00

Ortigia

Duomo, the lanes, Fonte Aretusa — a slow loop of the island before lunch.

13:00

Lunch at Caseificio Borderi

The legendary market sandwich counter: expect a line, it moves, and the wait is part of the show. One sandwich feeds a person and a half.

15:30

Plemmirio marine reserve

15 min south of Ortigia: swim and snorkel off the rocky coves of Punta della Mola. Crystal-clear protected water — bring water shoes and masks, entry is from the rocks.

Sunset

Back through Ortigia

Aperitivo on the seafront before the drive home, if there's energy left.

☀

Sunday logic: sandy beaches are at weekend peak — rocky Plemmirio absorbs a Sunday far better, and the snorkelling is the best in the area.

Mon 27Calamosche and Marianelli

Beach

morningmiddayevening

8:00

Calamosche at opening

The reserve's iconic cove, half empty on a Monday morning. 15–20 min walk from the lot on a rough track; bring lunch and water — there's nothing at the beach.

14:00

Marianelli

Calamosche's wilder twin, reachable on foot along the coast. Dunes, clear water, no services.

19:00

Marzamemi

Aperitivo and dinner in Piazza Regina Margherita — the fishing village at its liveliest on a summer evening. Book a table.

⚠

Two honest notes: Marianelli is the area's historic naturist beach (mixed crowd, no obligation). And leave nothing in the car — break-ins reported at the dirt parking lots.

Tue 28Cavagrande canyon

Nature

morningmiddayevening

8:00

Scala Cruci descent

~1h steep hike down into the canyon, swim in the chain of natural pools and small waterfalls, climb back out before noon.

Afternoon

Full rest

Shower, nap, pool. Quiet dinner in Noto. No beach today — the climb out is a workout and the legs earned it.

⛰

Non-negotiables: real sneakers (the trail wrecks sandals) and 2 litres of water each. If a strong scirocco hits a beach day, this canyon is the swap — it doesn't care about wind.

Wed 29Correnti, Carratois, Scicli and Modica

BeachCulture

morningmiddayevening

8:30

Isola delle Correnti

Sicily's southernmost tip, where the Ionian and the Mediterranean visibly meet. Quiet on a Wednesday morning; the swim between two seas is the photo of the trip.

11:30

Carratois

Five minutes up the coast: Caribbean-white sand, seafood lunch with feet in the sand at a lido, umbrella siesta. Fig granita stop in Portopalo on the way out (see Food).

17:00

Scicli passeggiata

~1h10 drive west: Via Mormino Penna and Palazzo Beneventano in the golden light, plus the town hall that plays Montalbano's police station. An easy, beautiful hour on foot.

20:00

Dinner in Modica

20 min up the gorge: dinner in the lower town under the floodlit Duomo staircase, chocolate for dessert, then ~50 min home.

🌬

Two notes: this is the big driving day (~2h30 total, split in three easy legs). And if a strong scirocco blows, the south-facing beaches turn rough — swap the morning with the Cavagrande day.

Eat this, there

One non-negotiable per town — the dish to try and where. Book dinners a day or two ahead in late July.

🍧

Noto

Granita di mandorla con brioche — Caffè Sicilia

Corso Vittorio Emanuele · breakfast, any day you're in town

Corrado Assenza's bar is a pilgrimage site: almond granita with warm brioche is the canonical Sicilian breakfast, and this is arguably its best version anywhere. For a proper dinner in Noto, Ristorante Crocifisso is the refined pick in the upper town.

🐟

Marzamemi

Red tuna and bottarga — La Cialoma

Piazza Regina Margherita · Monday dinner

The old tonnara village is tuna country: crudo, tuna steak, pasta with bottarga. La Cialoma on the piazza is the classic — book the outdoor table. Before leaving, stock up on jarred tuna and bottarga at the Campisi shop as edible souvenirs.

🥪

Ortigia

The market sandwich — Caseificio Borderi

Mercato di Ortigia · Sunday lunch (already in the plan)

Fresh mozzarella stretched in front of you, piled with whatever Damiano decides you need. The line is long and worth it. Next door, Fratelli Burgio does platters of local cheese and salumi if the group splits. Round it off with a glass of chilled Moscato di Siracusa.

🌿

Portopalo

Granita di fichi

Bars in the centre · Wednesday, leaving the beach

Late July is fig season, and Portopalo's fig granita is a cult local summer thing you won't find on the tourist trail — the perfect salty-skin snack between Carratois and the drive to Scicli. If figs are finished, gelsi (mulberry) is the backup of champions.

🥟

Scicli

Scacce ragusane, aperitivo on Via Mormino Penna

Wednesday, golden hour

The folded, stuffed flatbread of the Ragusa countryside — tomato and caciocavallo is the benchmark. Grab them from a bakery and eat them on the most beautiful street in town with a spritz before driving up to Modica.

🍫

Modica

Cold-worked chocolate — Antica Dolceria Bonajuto

Corso Umberto I · Wednesday after dinner

Sicily's oldest chocolate house (1880): grainy, Aztec-style bars in vanilla, cinnamon or chilli, plus the 'mpanatigghi — chocolate-and-meat pastries that sound wrong and taste right. They stay open late in summer, perfect after dinner in the lower town.
